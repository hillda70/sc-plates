[00]PREMISE

The first three Steps aren't theology. They're a procedure for changing a life — mapped by William James in 1902, turned into shared survival language by Bill Wilson in 1935, and independently confirmed by modern behavioral science.

[01]THE HANDOFF WAS LITERAL

December 1934. Days after his white-light experience at Towns Hospital, Wilson was handed a copy of The Varieties of Religious Experience. He read it cover to cover in that hospital bed. James gave him the map for what had just happened to him — collapse, surrender, transformation — and the license to judge the experience by its fruits rather than its roots.

Wilson never forgot the debt. He later declared that William James, dead since 1910, was a founder of Alcoholics Anonymous. A man who never heard of AA is on its founding roster — because the program's spine came out of his book.

[02]THE PROTOCOL
STEP 1 · "We admitted we were powerless…"

The old strategy collapses. Not a confession — an audit. What I've been doing isn't working, and the trying itself is what's breaking me.

JAMES deflation at depth · AA powerlessness · ACT creative hopelessness
STEP 2 · "Came to believe…"

Room for something new. A slow thaw, not a lightning bolt. You stop defending the old story and become the wider space where healing happens.

JAMES openness to "the More" · AA came to believe · ACT defusion / wider context
STEP 3 · "…God as we understood Him."

Pick a direction and move. Wilson built James directly into the step — the divine as an open variable. The only test: does relying on it keep you sober and whole?

JAMES divine as functional orientation · AA as we understood Him · ACT values + committed action
▲ RETAINED TENSION — DO NOT RESOLVE

For James and AA the power is encountered. For modern psychology it is constructed. The metaphysics doesn't matter. It works either way — and that's the proof of the system.

[03]EXECUTION

Stop running the strategy that's destroying you. Open to a new way. Commit to what matters — and let your life be the evidence.
